About Me

A little about my background and current work

I am a PhD Candidate in Applied Mathematics at the University of Houston, where I work under the supervision of Dr. Annalisa Quaini. My research sits at the intersection of mathematical modeling, scientific computing, and social phenomena — specifically, I develop agent-based and kinetic models to understand crowd dynamics and crime patterns in urban settings.

Before joining UH, I earned my Master's degree from the University of Dhaka with the highest distinction — receiving the A.F. Mujibur Rahman Gold Medal for First Class First — and subsequently taught mathematics at Primeasia University for four years, developing a deep passion for both research and pedagogy.

I am also an active member of the Association for Women in Mathematics (AWM) at UH, where I have served as Communication Chair and Treasurer, and I am committed to outreach and mentoring in the mathematical community.
